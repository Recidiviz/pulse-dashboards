By connecting with the right recovery resources, you can start building a support network and develop the skills needed to maintain long-term sobriety. # Stable Housing

Your current living situation is unpredictable and unsafe, which makes it harder for you to focus on your goals like staying on track with probation, finding stable work, and addressing other areas of your life. The good news is that there are resources and programs that can help you secure safe, stable housing both in the short term and the long term. Together, we’ll work on getting you into emergency housing quickly while also exploring programs that can help you transition into affordable, more permanent housing options.

## Short-Term Housing Stability (1-4 weeks)
1. **Apply for Emergency Housing (Week 1-2):**
   - Contact [City Housing Services](#e4eaaaf2-d142-11e1-b3e4-080027620d20) and [Homestead Housing Support](#e4eaaaf2-d142-11e1-b3e4-080027620d21) to ask about emergency housing or shelter availability.
   - Complete their housing applications and follow up with staff to ensure progress.
   - **Goal:** By the end of week 2, you should have a temporary, safe place to stay through emergency housing or a shelter program.

2. **Eligibility Review for Housing Programs (Week 2-4):**
   - Work with your caseworker to determine eligibility for longer-term housing assistance, such as Section 8 or similar subsidized programs.
   - Start gathering the required paperwork, such as ID and proof of probation, to prepare for application submissions.
   - **Goal:** By the end of week 4, complete the eligibility steps and submit applications for long-term housing support.

## Long-Term Housing Plan (1-4 months)
1. **Transition to Stable Housing (Month 2-3):**
   - Once approved for housing assistance, begin the process of moving into a transitional program or affordable rental unit.
   - Start budgeting for necessary expenses like rent, food, and transportation with help from your caseworker if needed.
   - **Goal:** By month 3, you should have moved into stable housing and started a routine of managing daily responsibilities in your new living space.

2. **Create a Long-Term Sustainability Plan (Month 3-4):**
   - Work closely with your caseworker to develop a set plan to maintain housing, including rent payments, saving for the future, and resolving housing-related challenges.
   - Begin practicing long-term self-sufficiency skills, like improving your credit score or building a housing stability fund.
   - **Goal:** By month 4, you should have a clear and achievable plan for keeping your housing stabilized independently.
