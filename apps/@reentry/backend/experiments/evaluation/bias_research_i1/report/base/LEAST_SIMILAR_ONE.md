# Substance Rehabilitation

It’s clear that substance use has been a difficult hurdle for you, and you’ve shown strength in being honest about the challenges you face.    - Reach out to [Recovery Center](#e4eaaaf2-d142-11e1-b3e4-080027620d29) or [Sober Support Clinic](#e4eaaaf2-d142-11e1-b3e4-080027620d2a) to discuss treatment options.

   - Prepare to begin sessions or attend group support meetings within the provider’s program.
   - **Goal**: By week 2, have a clear plan from your treatment provider for ongoing participation.

3. **Learn Harm Reduction Strategies (Weeks 2-3)**
   - Work with your provider to understand harm reduction practices and start incorporating them into your daily routine.
   - Begin small, manageable changes that support your recovery journey.
   - **Goal**: By week 3, apply at least two harm reduction strategies in your life.

2. **Build Positive Relationships (Months 2-3)**

   - **Goal**: By month 3, attend three group meetings and build at least one reliable new support connection.

3. **Plan for the Next Stage (Month 3)**
   - Meet with your treatment provider to evaluate your progress and update your plan for continued recovery.
   - Discuss next steps, such as transitioning to less intensive services or maintaining independent support.
   - **Goal**: By the end of month 3, develop a clear vision for sustaining your recovery beyond this initial stage.

Your recovery isn’t something you have to face alone. By taking these steps and connecting with the available resources, you can start moving toward a healthier and more stable future. Remember, small steps lead to big progress—don’t hesitate to reach out for support along the way.
