# Milestones

## Criminal Activity Safety (Week 4)
You should have a clear understanding of your legal options and rights after discussing with legal assistance. This will help you make informed decisions about disengaging from gang activity.

## Criminal Activity Safety (Month 3)
You should be relocating to a safer, gang-free living environment that reduces the risks of violence and ongoing criminal exposure. This will be a major step toward improving your safety and stability.

## Substance Rehabilitation (Month 2)
You should have completed at least 8 outpatient sessions, actively working on tools to manage substance cravings and stress. This progress will signal that you’re building consistency in your recovery process.

## Substance Rehabilitation (Month 3)
You should have attended at least 4 peer support or recovery group sessions, demonstrating disciplined follow-through on your treatment goals.

## Stable Housing (Month 3)
You should have secured a long-term, stable housing situation—with ongoing support to ensure it meets safety and stability needs. This outcome will create a foundation for focusing on other areas of your life.

## Stable Housing (Month 6)
You should have a housing stability plan in place to ensure you can continue to manage rent and other responsibilities, reducing the chance of facing instability again.

## Skill-Based Employment (Month 4)
You should be enrolled or actively participating in a vocational training program or certification course that aligns with your skills. This indicates you’re progressing toward long-term employment opportunities.

## Skill-Based Employment (Month 5)
You should have transitioned into a stable part-time or full-time job—or be well-advanced in completing a training program. This progress will help establish financial independence and self-sufficiency.

## Health Management (Month 2)
All urgent physical health issues, including your respiratory and dental concerns, should be actively managed or treated. This will signal that your most serious health barriers are being addressed.

## Health Management (Month 6)
You should have developed at least one consistent, manageable stress-reduction practice that you’re using regularly. This will help maintain overall mental health and emotional stability.

## Positive Relationships (Month 2)
You should have initiated contact with at least one family member or trusted individual—helping build or rebuild connections for emotional support. This will encourage positive social interactions.

## Positive Relationships (Month 3)
You should have participated regularly in a supportive counseling program, focusing on breaking away from harmful influences and fostering healthier relationships.

## Structured Leisure (Month 3)
You should have participated in at least one structured activity or hobby that provides a positive and safe environment. This will help you redirect your energy toward healthier and more meaningful pursuits.

## Structured Leisure (Month 6)
You should have established a recurring involvement in an activity or program based on your interests, providing a constructive outlet and reinforcing stability in your routine.

3. **Substance Rehabilitation:** Attend intake evaluation at Recovery Center and set a schedule for outpatient sessions. 4. **Health Management:** Complete the health evaluation and establish an immediate care plan.

## Week 3
5. **Positive Relationships:** Begin first counseling appointment. Discuss goals related to creating a supportive network.

## Week 4
