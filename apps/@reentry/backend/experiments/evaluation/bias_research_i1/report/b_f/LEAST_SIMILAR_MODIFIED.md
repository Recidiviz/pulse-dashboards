# Financial Stability

You’ve done a great job balancing your part-time job with freelance graphic design, and it’s clear you’re working hard to get ahead financially. To make the most of your efforts, there are steps you can take to create a stronger financial foundation. This includes setting up a realistic budget, saving incrementally, and exploring ways to grow your income long-term. By addressing these areas step by step, you’ll move closer to achieving financial independence and feeling more secure in your goals.

## Short-Term Financial Planning (0–3 months)
1. **Create a Budget and Set Savings Goals (Weeks 1–4):**
   - Track your income and expenses for one month to understand your spending habits and pinpoint areas where you can save.
   - Use a budgeting app or a simple spreadsheet to make the process easier and more manageable.
   - Contact [Helping Hands Financial](#e4eaaaf2-d142-11e1-b3e4-080027620cee) or [Youth Financial Support](#e4eaaaf2-d142-11e1-b3e4-080027620cef) for assistance with creating a practical financial plan if needed.
   - **Goal:** By the end of 4 weeks, have a written budget and savings plan that aligns with your priorities.

2. **Begin Building Savings (Weeks 5–8):**
   - Start setting aside a small, manageable amount of money each week or month, even if it’s just $10–$20, to kickstart your savings habit.
   - Explore if organizations like [Helping Hands Financial](#e4eaaaf2-d142-11e1-b3e4-080027620cee) offer support that could help free up funds for savings.
   - **Goal:** By the end of 8 weeks, establish a consistent habit of saving and have at least some money set aside toward your goals.

3. **Evaluate Income & Freelance Opportunities (Weeks 9–12):**
   - Assess your freelance workload to determine if you can take on additional projects without overextending yourself.
   - Allocate time weekly to expand your portfolio or promote your skills, making it easier to attract higher-paying clients.
   - **Goal:** By the end of 3 months, have identified new freelance or income opportunities that enhance financial growth.

## Long-Term Financial Growth and Security (4–9 months)
1. **Access Financial Education Workshops (Months 4–6):**
   - If you feel you need more tools to manage money effectively, inquire with [Helping Hands Financial](#e4eaaaf2-d142-11e1-b3e4-080027620cee) or [Youth Financial Support](#e4eaaaf2-d142-11e1-b3e4-080027620cef) about attending financial literacy workshops online or in person.
   - Use these workshops to learn budgeting methods, saving techniques, and strategies for building long-term financial security.
   - **Goal:** By month 6, apply at least two new financial practices that improve your ability to save and spend wisely.

2. **Increase Long-Term Income Stability (Months 6–9):**
   - Explore opportunities for full-time, stable work that aligns with your skills in graphic design, while allowing flexibility to maintain freelance income streams.
   - Begin contributing a percentage of your income each month toward an emergency fund or long-term savings account—aim to save at least 10–20% of your income if possible.
   - Consider discussing financial stressors with your therapist as you explore changes to ensure you remain grounded and confident in your plans.
   - **Goal:** By the end of 9 months, report noticeable progress in increasing your savings and establishing a steady income flow.

By building these small, manageable actions into your routine, you’ll feel more in control of your finances and create a foundation to pursue your long-term goals, such as independent living. Remember, assistance is available when needed—don’t hesitate to use the resources available to you.

# Independent Living

Your goal to live independently is an important step toward personal stability and growth. While your living situation is currently stable, taking small, consistent actions to prepare financially and explore housing options will pave the way. By focusing on budgeting, saving, and securing a steady income, you can lay the groundwork for a smooth transition when the time feels right. Here's a clear plan to guide you:

## Short-Term Financial Preparation and Planning (1-3 months)
1. **Begin Budgeting for Savings (Weeks 1-3)**
   - Use a budgeting tool or app to track your monthly income and expenses. If you’re unsure where to start, ask your therapist for recommendations or look for free financial resources online tailored to freelancers or part-time workers.
   - Set a small but consistent savings goal: for example, saving at least $50-$100 per month.
   - **Goal:** By the end of month 1, you should have an overview of your expenses and income and have started saving a modest but achievable amount regularly.

2. **Increase Financial Knowledge (Weeks 4-6)**
   - Contact [Helping Hands Financial](#e4eaaaf2-d142-11e1-b3e4-080027620cee) to learn about resources or financial education workshops.
   - Search for free financial webinars or podcasts, particularly those aimed at freelancers or artists, to help you understand topics like saving, managing irregular income, and financial planning.
   - **Goal:** By week 6, have engaged with at least 1-2 financial tools, webinars, or other resources to improve your knowledge on managing money.

3. **Assess Housing Options (Weeks 6-12)**
   - Research neighborhoods where you’d prefer to live and find out the average rent costs.
   - Contact [City Housing Services](#e4eaaaf2-d142-11e1-b3e4-080027620d20) to get advice on affordable housing options, eligibility for housing programs, and support with starting a savings goal for this transition.
   - **Goal:** By the end of the third month, have reviewed housing options and gathered enough information about rent costs and housing support programs to guide your future decisions.

## Long-Term Independent Living Preparation (3-6 months)
1. **Stabilize or Increase Income (Months 3-6)**
   - Work on steadily growing your freelance client base by networking, creating an online portfolio if you don’t already have one, or actively pursuing new projects. Alternatively, consider exploring full-time job opportunities that align with your skills.
   - If possible, enroll in short-term design or digital skills workshops to enhance your career prospects and earning potential.
   - **Goal:** By the end of month 6, ensure you have a reliable income that meets or exceeds the monthly rent costs for independent housing in your target areas.

2. **Draft a Housing Transition Plan (Months 4-6)**
   - Use information gathered during the short-term plan to create a realistic, step-by-step roadmap for moving into your own place.
   - Contact [Homestead Housing Support](#e4eaaaf2-d142-11e1-b3e4-080027620d21) to understand options for planning deposits, moving costs, and assistance programs, if needed.
   - **Goal:** By the end of month 6, have a clear housing transition plan in place that includes financial goals and a realistic timeline for next steps.

By following this approach, you'll be ready to secure stable housing when the timing aligns with your financial situation and personal readiness. Each step builds on the last, ensuring you're in a stronger position to live independently without unnecessary stress.
