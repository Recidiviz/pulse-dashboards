   - **Goal**: By Month 4, finalize a clear plan for maintaining independent, stable housing.

By working through this timeline, we’ll make sure your housing situation moves from temporary and unsafe to long-term and secure. Take these steps one at a time, and keep in close contact with your caseworker or housing counselor when you need help or guidance to keep things moving forward.

## Week 2
- **Substance Program Participation**: Attend initial intake appointment and define treatment plan for recovery.
- **Document Organization**: Collate required materials for housing applications.
- **Mental Health Support**: Arrange an initial consultation with Northside Counseling Services.

## Week 3
- **Start Recovery Engagement**: Begin active participation in selected recovery programs (e.g., group meetings).
- **Submit Housing Applications**: Finalize initial applications for temporary housing options.
- **Therapy Introduction**: Attend and engage in your first mental health therapy session.

## Week 4
- **Temporary Housing Moving**: Transition into secured temporary housing if provided.
- **Health Follow-Up**: Attend scheduled health screening and discuss essential medical care needs.
- **Routine Recovery Activities**: Maintain consistent attendance in group recovery meetings.

## Months 2-3
- **Housing Advancement**: Apply to transitional housing programs for future stability.
- **Vocational Steps**: Enroll in a vocational training course such as CDL or HVAC certification.
- **Family Connection Attempts**: Reach out to family for initiating reconciliation and planning safe interactions.

## Months 4-6
- **Complete Certification**: Finalize training programs and begin structured job applications with career guidance.
- **Secure Long-Term Housing**: Confirm residency in an approved housing unit.
- **Strengthen Family Bonds**: Participate in joint family activities to rebuild trust and support networks.
