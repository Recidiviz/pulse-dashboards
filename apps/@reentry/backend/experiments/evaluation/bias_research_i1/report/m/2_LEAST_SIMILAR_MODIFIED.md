   - **Goal**: By the end of week 6, identify at least one family counseling or mediation program to explore further.

3. **Lean on Your Support Network (Weeks 1-6)**
   - Engage with close friends, your roommate, or trusted connections in your art communities for emotional support while working through this process. Use these connections to talk through challenges or just decompress.
   - **Goal**: By week 6, check in with yourself and your support system to ensure they’re helping you stay balanced and that you’re using them effectively.

## Long-Term Rebuilding Relationships (3-5 months)

1. **Initiate Family Counseling or Mediation (Months 3-4)**
   - Once you’ve identified a resource and feel emotionally ready, schedule and attend an initial family session through counseling or mediation. Work with your therapist beforehand to set expectations and identify what you hope to achieve in the first session.

2. **Reflect and Adjust with Your Therapist (Months 4-5)**

   - **Goal**: By month 5, have a clear understanding of the state of your family relationships and next steps, supported by your therapist’s insight.

3. **Maintain Emotional Balance and Growth (Months 3-5)**
   - Alongside family work, stay consistent with your therapy sessions and mindfulness practices to manage stress. Check in with your friends and art communities as needed to regularly seek balance and joy.
   - **Goal**: By month 5, review how you’ve maintained emotional balance while navigating family reconciliation.

This plan is flexible and focused on progress, not perfection. With each step, you’re building a stronger foundation for meaningful social connections, both with your family and your existing support network. Let’s take it one step at a time—you’re not alone in this process.
