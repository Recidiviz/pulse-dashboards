PO: Come on in, Arthur. Grab a seat.

Client: Appreciate it. [Clears throat] Woo, that wind is kicking up out there today.

PO: It really is. Supposed to storm later, I think. How've you been holding up since I saw you last?

Client: Oh, you know me. Just, uh... keeping my head down. Same old, same old. Nothin' new to report on my end.

PO: Glad to hear it. Quiet is good. 

Client: Yes sir. Now, didn't you tell me you were heading up to the lake this past weekend? Get any fishing in?

PO: I did, actually. Fired up the truck Friday evening. But I'll tell you what, Arthur, the fish were not cooperating.

Client: [Laughs] Is that right? Man, they never do when you actually want 'em to. 

PO: Spent six hours on the water Saturday morning. Used every lure in the tackle box. Nothing but a couple of tiny bluegill.

Client: See, that's your problem right there. You out there messing with them plastic lures. You gotta use live bait if you want the big ones to bite.

PO: I brought worms! They just kept stealing 'em right off the hook.

Client: Well, they smarten up, you know? Specially this time of year. Water gets a little cooler, they start... they just sit down there at the bottom staring at you.

PO: Felt like they were laughing at me, honestly.

Client: Oh, they definitely were. [Crosstalk]

PO: [Crosstalk] Just mocking me from the reeds.

Client: Shoot, I used to sit out by the creek with my uncle, and we wouldn't catch a single thing all day. Just sunburned and hungry. But it beats being inside, I guess.

PO: That’s what I kept telling myself. At least it was peaceful. Until I got back to the cabin and put the football game on.

Client: Oh, don't even get me started. Did you watch that mess on Saturday?

PO: I did. I couldn't believe my eyes in the third quarter.

Client: Man, I was hollering at the television. I almost knocked over my iced tea. What was the coach thinking with that play call on fourth down?

PO: Running it right up the middle against that defensive line? Made no sense.

Client: No sense at all! You got a quarterback who can throw fifty yards, and you hand it off to a boy who weighs a buck forty soaking wet. It's... it's just stubbornness, is what it is.

PO: Stubborn is the right word for it. Coach just wouldn't adjust his game plan.

Client: He never does. Reminds me of... well, shoot, it reminds me of my mama, Lord rest her.

PO: How's that?

Client: Because once she made her mind up about how something was gonna go down, ain't no force on this earth was gonna change it. You couldn't talk no sense into that woman.

PO: Sounds like a strong-willed lady.

Client: Oh, she was something else. She was the head of the usher board at Mount Zion for, oh, thirty-odd years. Wore them big hats, you know? Had the white gloves. You didn't mess with her.

PO: I wouldn't dare.

Client: I remember one time—this gotta be back in, oh, eighty-eight or eighty-nine. We had this big fat raccoon that kept getting into the trash cans behind the house.

PO: Those things can be a real nuisance. And mean, too.

Client: Mean as a junkyard dog! So, my brother and I, we were terrified of it. We told her, "Mama, call animal control. That thing's got rabies or something." But she wasn't trying to hear it.

PO: So what did she do?

Client: Man, she waited out on the back porch one night in her housecoat. Holding the big straw broom.

PO: [Laughs] Just waiting in ambush?

Client: Just sitting in the dark! And sure enough, here comes Mr. Raccoon, waddling up to the garbage can. She flips on the porch light and just charges at him.

PO: With the broom?

Client: Sweeping at him like he was a pile of dust! Yelling, "Get on out of here! You don't pay no rent!"

PO: [Laughing] Did he run?

Client: Run? He looked at her for about two seconds, hissed, and then she clocked him right between the ears with the bristle end. He scrambled over that chain-link fence so fast, I ain't never seen an animal move like that. We never saw him again.

PO: That is incredible. She handled it herself.

Client: That's what I'm saying about the coach! You gotta be decisive. Mama didn't hesitate, she just brought the broom. Coach out there hesitating on fourth and two.

PO: Maybe they need to hire your mama to run the offense.

Client: Listen, she'd have whipped them boys into shape, I guarantee you that. They wouldn't be jumping offsides if they had a broom waiting on 'em on the sideline.

PO: [Laughs] You're probably right about that. Man, they really blew it this weekend.

Client: Sure did. But, you know... there's always next Saturday, right?

PO: Always next Saturday. Let's hope they figure out their play calling by then.

Client: I ain't holding my breath, Mr. Davis. But I'll be watching. I always do.

PO: Well, Arthur, it is always a pleasure catching up. I see my next appointment just pulled into the parking lot outside.

Client: Oh, well, let me get out of your hair then. 

PO: No rush. You have a good rest of your week, stay warm out there in this wind.

Client: I sure will. You do the same now. And next time you go fishing, get you some crickets or something. Leave them plastic worms at the house.

PO: I'll take your advice on that. Take care, Arthur.

Client: Alright now. Have a good one.
