## What it means:

The CSED is the date when your supervision ends, and you are fully discharged from ADCRR custody.

## Who has it:

Most people with sentences after 1994 that require community supervision.

## Criteria:

Stay compliant with supervision conditions.
Avoid violations that could extend supervision (e.g., absconding).

## Implications:

After CSED, you are no longer under state supervision. If you abscond or refuse to sign conditions, your CSED may move later, but never beyond the SED.
