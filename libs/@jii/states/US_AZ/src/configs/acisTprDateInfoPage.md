## What it means:

The Standard Transition Program allows eligible people to leave prison up to 90 days before their earliest eligible release date (often but not always CSBD), if they are willing to attend a program while in the community. It is designed to help people transition to the community earlier.

## Who has it:

Most people who do not have disqualifying convictions can qualify if they meet the criteria (see below).

## What do I need to do in order to be released on this date?:

Some people will not be able to qualify for a Transition Release Program because of their current offense or criminal history.

- Disqualifying offenses (sex offenses, arson, or certain violent crimes – see ARS 13-901.03).
  If those do not apply to you, then you may be able to qualify for STP if you do the following:
- You must sign the Transition Program agreement form.
- You must be on Medium or Minimum custody.
- You must meet the Mandatory Literacy requirement, unless you have an exemption.
- You can’t have any felony holds, felony detainers, or felony warrants.
- You can’t be found guilty of any major disciplinary infractions within 6 months of your CSBD / TR to ADD. Even if you qualified, this will cause you to lose your TPR date.
- You cannot refuse or have been removed from a major program within 18 months of your CSBD / TR to ADD.
- You must have an approved home plan – starting to work on this ASAP is essential to receiving your full 90 days.
- You can’t have any past arrests, convictions, or requirements to register for sex offenses.
- You can’t have a history of Dangerous Crimes Against Children (ARS 13-705).
- You can’t have been revoked to prison after release while serving your current sentence.
- You must be a US Citizen or legal permanent resident without an ICE detainer.

## If I’m released on STP, what does that mean for me?:

With TPR, you begin community supervision early. During the TPR period, you will need to regularly attend a program in the community, and report to an officer.
