The Parole Eligibility Date (PED) only exists for individuals who may be eligible for parole. The Nebraska Board of Parole sets Parole Hearing Dates based on the outcomes of reviews. At the hearing, the Nebraska Board of Parole will make a decision on whether to grant parole.

How PED is calculated depends on the individuals’ sentence terms.

For individuals serving a mandatory minimum sentence, the Parole Eligibility Date will never be before the mandatory minimum term date.

The date is otherwise the earliest date of the three following options:

- (a) When the person has served one-half the minimum term of his or her sentence
- (b) If serving max term of less than 20 years, two years prior to the person’s TRD
- (c) If serving a max term of greater than 20 years, when the person has served eighty percent of the time until the TRD

Changes from LB 50 have already been reflected in dates.
