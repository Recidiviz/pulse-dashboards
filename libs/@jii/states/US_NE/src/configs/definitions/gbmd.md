## What it is

GB/MD (Good Time Balance and Mandatory Discharge) days are issued upfront, all at once, to reduce your Tentative Release Date (TRD). This is reflected in your TRD upon intake.

## How it works

If you don’t have a Mandatory Minimum, you receive GB/MD days equal to half of your maximum sentence immediately. For example, if you don’t have a Mandatory Minimum Term Date and your maximum sentence is 8 years, you would receive GB/MD days equal to 4 years right away, reducing your TRD accordingly.

If you have served your Mandatory Minimum Term Date, you receive GB/MD days equal to half of your remaining maximum sentence. For example, if your maximum sentence is 10 years and you have served your Mandatory Minimum Term of 4 years, and 6 years remain, you would receive GB/MD days equal to 3 years.

## Losing Good Time: Restorable / Non-Restorable

This good time may be lost from misconduct reports. In some cases, you may be eligible to restore any days that were lost. If you have restorable lost days, speak with your case manager to understand when you can apply to have those credits restored.
