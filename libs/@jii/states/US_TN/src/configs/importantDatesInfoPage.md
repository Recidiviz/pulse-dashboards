## Release Eligibility Date

Your Release Eligibility Date (RED) is the first day you can become eligible to see the Parole Board. Being “eligible” means the Board may choose to grant you parole, but it does not guarantee release. Around your RED, you will be scheduled for a **Parole Hearing Date** — this is the actual date when you go in front of the Board. From that point on, the hearing date is what matters for your release, not the RED. If the Board does not grant parole at your hearing, they will set another hearing date in the future.

## What does the percent (%) served mean?

Every parole‑eligible sentence has a percent. This is the amount of your sentence you must serve before you are first eligible for parole. Example: A 10‑year sentence at 30% means you would reach RED after 3 years. If it were a 60% sentence, you would reach RED after 6 years.

## Can my Release Eligibility Date move sooner?

Sentence Credits can move your RED earlier. For most people, credits can reduce the time to RED by **up to 30%**. If the crime you are serving time for happened **before January 1, 1988**, credits can reduce that time by **up to 35%**. If your sentence has a mandatory minimum (for example, 85% or 100% sentence), other rules may limit how much sooner the RED can move.

## Expiration Date
The Expiration Date (EXP) is when your sentence ends. You will be released on this date if you are not already on Parole. Sentence Credits may or may not move this date – it depends on your case:
* If your offense was **before July 1, 2024**: Sentence Credits can reduce EXP.
* If your offense was **on/after July 1, 2024** and your sentence is **under 2 years**: Sentence Credits can reduce EXP.
* If your offense was on/after **July 1, 2024** and your sentence is **2 years or more**: Sentence Credits do **not** change EXP.

If the Judge granted you Jail Credits for time spent in County Jail before sentencing, those also reduce your EXP. Without Sentence Credits or Jail Credits, your Expiration Date would be the same as your Full Expiration.

## Full Expiration

Full Expiration (FXP) is the very last possible day you could be held in prison on the sentence. FXP does **not** change with credits. It is what your Expiration Date would be without Sentence Credits.

For example, if a person started a 10-year sentence on January 1, 2020, their FXP would be December 31, 2029.

## Safety Valve (SV)

Safety Valve (SV) is an early release date the state may use to ease overcrowding. It is set using a formula and can move earlier if your RED moves. Not everyone has an SV, and it is not used often.
