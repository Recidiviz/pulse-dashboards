## What it is

The GB/MD (Good Time Balance and Mandatory Discharge) days are issued up front. This is reflected in your TRD (Tentative Release Date) upon intake.

## How it works

If you don’t have a Mandatory Minimum sentence, you receive six months for each year of your term and pro rata (proportion) for any part of your term which is less than a year.

If you have a Mandatory Minimum sentence, your GB/MD days will reflect good time based on your Mandatory Minimum Term Date (MMTD).

## Losing Good Time: Restorable / Non-Restorable

This good time can be removed for Misconduct Reports (MRs).

## How to Get Time Back

If you lost restorable time, you can earn it back by following the rules. **NDCS staff will now automatically start this process** for you within one month of you becoming eligible.

## Can I Get My Time Back?

This is a summary of the policy to **restore lost good time**. For the official legal rules and information on how to appeal a decision, please refer to **NDCS Policy 117.02** in the Law Library. LB 191 and other forms of Good Time are different, and have separate policies.

To be eligible to restore lost good time, you must meet these rules:

- **No guilty finding of "Class I" Misconduct** for the last 1 year
- **No "IDC" Misconduct** for the last 6 months.
- **No more than 2 "UDC" Misconducts** for the last 6 months.
  - **Note:** These time limits start from the date of your last misconduct report.
- **Stay out of Long Term Restrictive Housing:** You cannot be in LTRH now, and cannot have been there in the last 90 days.
- **Participate in Treatment:** You must accept and go to all clinical programs recommended for you.
- **Time Left:** Your tentative release date (TRD) must be more than 4 months away.

## How the Process Works

1. **Staff Starts the Request:** Once you meet the rules, a staff member will put in the request for you.
2. **The Decision:** The Warden will approve or deny the request within 20 days.
3. **Getting More Time:** Initially, you can get 30 days back at a time, once a month.

   - If you get 30 days back every month for 5 months in a row and you still have some lost good time left, you can then start getting 60 days back each month.
   - After three months of 60-day restorations, you may be eligible for 90 days back each month.

4. **If You Get a New Misconduct Report:** If you stop meeting the rules, the process resets. You will have to go back to earning 30 days at a time for 5 months before you can get more again.
5. **If Denied:** If your request is turned down, staff will wait 90 days before trying again.
