The Parole Eligibility Date (PED) is the date you are first eligible for parole. The Nebraska Board of Parole sets Parole Hearing Dates based on the outcomes of reviews. At the hearing, the Nebraska Board of Parole will make a decision on whether to grant parole, deny parole, or defer the decision to a later date.

Your PED depends on your sentence terms and any changes to your good time.

A mandatory minimum sentence is the minimum time you must serve before becoming eligible for parole. Your Parole Eligibility Date can never be before the mandatory minimum sentence date.

You are eligible for parole upon the earliest date of the following:

- (a) “When the person has served one-half the minimum term of his or her sentence as provided in Nebraska Revised Statute §§ 83-1,107 and 83-1,108”;
- (b) “If serving max term of 20 years or less, two years prior to the person’s mandatory discharge date” (Tentative Release Date); or
- (c) “If serving a max term of more than 20 years, when the person has served eighty percent of the time until the person’s mandatory discharge date” (Tentative Release Date)

If your parole eligibility date or tentative release date is changed, you will be given written notice of the new tentative release date or parole eligibility date.

Changes related to parole eligibility dates from LB 50 have already been reflected in dates.
