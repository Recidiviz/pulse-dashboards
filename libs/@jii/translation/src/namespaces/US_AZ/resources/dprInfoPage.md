Because of your specific charges, you **may** qualify for a sentence structure that allows you to leave prison sooner. This is called Drug Program Release (DPR).

Right now, your release dates are calculated based on serving 85.7% of your sentence. However, if you are eligible and you complete a **Major Program**, you can earn release credits faster. This would allow you to be released after serving only **70%** of your sentence.

**To get these earlier dates, you must finish a Major Program.** Until you finish the program, your dates will stay the same.

## What do I need to do?

You must complete two steps to secure these earlier dates:

### 1. FINISH A MAJOR PROGRAM

You must complete a program that qualifies for DPR.

### 2. AVOID DISCIPLINARY INFRACTIONS

You must keep earning your release credits by avoiding disciplinary tickets.

## How do I find a Major Program?

Not every program counts. To see the official list of major programs:

1. Go to the FYI App on your tablet.
1. Open the Departmental Orders section.
1. Open DO1002 and look at Attachment E.

## Who can possibly qualify for DPR?

You can earn a DPR date if your sentence is for only one of these drug possession or use offenses:

- Possession or use of Marijuana (A.R.S. §13-3405(A)(1))
- Possession or use of Dangerous Drug (A.R.S. §13-3407(A)(1))
- Possession or use of a Narcotic Drug (A.R.S. §13-3408(A)(1))
- Possession or use of Drug Paraphernalia (A.R.S. §13-3415(A))

You also cannot have a past conviction for a violent or aggravated felony.

## What do I need to do to be released on DPR?

DPR can move your release dates sooner, but it does not change the requirements to be released on that date. To learn more about the rules for each release type, see this page.

## What else should I know?

If you qualify for DPR, you might also qualify for the [Drug Transition Program, or DTP](../more-information/important-dates#acisDtpDateRaw). This could let you leave up to 90 days earlier.
