## What it means:

A release for people who can earn 3 credits for every 7 days they serve (“70% sentences”).

## Who has it?:

People who are convicted only of drug possession/use charges. There are 4 specific crimes that qualify.

## Criteria:

- You must be convicted of only the following offenses
  - Possession or use of Marijuana (13-3405(A)(1))
  - Possession or use of Dangerous Drug (13-3407(A)(1))
  - Possession or use of a Narcotic Drug(13-3408(A)(1))
  - Possession or use of Drug Paraphernalia (13-3415(A))
- You must complete a drug treatment program or other major program during your current incarceration.
- You cannot have been convicted of a violent or aggravated felony in the past.

## What would it mean for me if I’m released on this date?:

DTP release works like an ERCD release. You would serve the rest of your sentence on Community Supervision in the community. Conditions of Supervision apply, including reporting to an officer and possibly electronic monitoring.
